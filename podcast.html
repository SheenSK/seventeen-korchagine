Therapy Podcast Templates

InDesign · Figma · 2026

This project presents three possible visual identities for a podcast focusing on mental health, run by therapists and psychiatrists. The mockups are designed in figma - they act as templates that the customer can re-use indefinitely after the purchase. The core visuals are built around public domain image archives and open source fonts. This way, the customer can customize the template with a stock of images at their disposal, without having to worry about licensing.

Images: Public Domain Image Archive
Fonts: Robota, Elite (Google Open Font License)

[INQUIRE]
— Concept One —

Initial explorations focused on creating a visual language that bridges medical precision with human warmth. The typewriter aesthetic references the history of medical documentation while remaining accessible.

— Concept Two —

This version acts as a callback to the visual language used by the British documentarian Adam Curtis, whom the podcast cites as an inspiration. This identity pays homage to Curtis, while still feeling distinct.

— Concept Three —

The final template uses warm, calming colors inspired by the Podcast's website. This design picks up the human-centered approach of accepting imperfection as presented in the podcast.

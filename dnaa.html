OASIS:TooGoodToGo

University Project · Blender · 2024

This pop-up store for the food-sharing service Too Good To Go creates a vision of a better future, one in which nature and technology are codependent. An oasis of hope opens up in our world, a futuristic space where customers can create their own climate-neutral meal and take it home as a testament to a brighter, eco-friendlier future

— Walkthrough —

Attracted by the glow of the vertical farms, the customer enters the pop-up store. They are greeted by an empoyee, who explains the concept of the pop-up. The customer proceeds to the table (the most well-lit object in the room). They fill a reusable container with leftovers of their choice and add fresh herbs from the vertical farms to it. The fresh herbs transform the leftovers into a gourmet meal. The store can also function as a gallery space, as the customer learns about the concept of vertical farming by reading the information plaques on the walls. The customer then leaves the store.

— Light —

The store uses light to guide the customer to the buffet table, which is the main attraction in the room. The subtle glow of the farms creates an atmosphere of mystery, of an unknown new world

— Building Blocks —

The concept contains only three building blocks: Three vertical farms, one buffet table, and a shelf suspended above the table. None of the elements are connected to the walls, and can be rearranged at will.

— Materials —

The store uses highly reective materials to convey a feeling of cleanliness and efficiency. The door is made of pure concrete, which helps save costs while conveying a brutalistic feeling
